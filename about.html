About

Every argument comes from somewhere. This show declares where.

Host

Ruari Mears — independent analyst. Video and audio under the channel name Ruari Mears; the series title is A Common Enemy.

This is a personal project, separate from RMS Ltd. (worker-owned tech cooperative in formation).

Declared standpoint

I am a democratic socialist. I grew up inside the socialist labour movement tradition — not as metaphor, but as formation. That gives me specific analytical tools and specific blind spots. The purpose of naming them is not apology; it is methodology.

The show applies a steelman obligation, a bias audit, and a three-register system (evidence → interpretation → moral argument) to keep analysis legible under scrutiny — especially from listeners who do not share my starting point.

Full treatment: Episode 0, Segment 1 — The Declared Standpoint.

What this project is

A Common Enemy argues that structural convergence in contemporary British politics is visible through a six-axis framework — grounded in primary sources, peer-reviewed literature, and propositional logic — presented for a general audience.

Companion framing: Daniel Trilling's If We Tolerate This documents what happened to the mainstreaming of the far right. This show argues why it happened and what it means structurally.
